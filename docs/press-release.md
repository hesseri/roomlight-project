# Roomlight
**Universal brand consistency meets personalized guest comfort—lighting as a service.**


## Problem and Solution
Travelers are often tired of confusing array of switches found in most hotel rooms around the world. And at the same time, global hotel chains struggle with inconsistent atmospheres between different hotels in the same chain. Traditional lighting control systems are either too rigid for the guest or too complex for the hotel staff to maintain consistently across diverse room layouts.

Roomlight is the bridge between professional interior design and personal guest comfort."


## How to get started
1. Map Your Rooms: Tag your in-room hardware (e.g., bedside lamps as 'Task', ceiling LEDs as 'Ambient') using the Roomlight Setup App.

2. Define Your Brand: Create your signature themes, like Golden Hour or Energizing Morning — in the Cloud Dashboard.

3. Deploy Globally: Hit 'Sync' to push these themes to every Roomlight controller in your network, regardless of the room size or bulb count.

4. Guests can immediately use the presets or enter 'Flex Mode' to fine-tune their environment with the Brightness and Warmth sliders.

## Optional FAQ
**What happens if a suite has 12 lights but a standard room only has 6?**
> This is handled by our tagging system. Instead of controlling specific bulbs, our themes control categories like *Ambient*, *Task*, and *Accent*. The system automatically maps the theme to whatever hardware is available.

**Does the guest lose the preset theme if they use the custom sliders?**
> Not at all. If a guest adjusts the sliders in Flex Mode, the system treats it as a temporary override. The UI indicates that they are in "Flex Mode." They can return to the perfectly calibrated brand presets at any time with a single tap on the theme buttons.

**Is the system dependent on a constant cloud connection?**
> No. While themes and updates are synced via the cloud, each Roomlight controller stores its mapping data and theme logic locally. This ensures that even if the hotel's internet goes down, the lights and the guest experience remain fully functional.
