# RoomLight — Prototype Scope

The RoomLight prototype is a single working hotel room that lets us validate the product's core assumptions before committing to a full rollout. It is aimed at decision-makers and operations managers who need to see the concept proven in a real environment.

The prototype sets out to answer three questions: Can a zone-based theme approach deliver a consistent branded atmosphere regardless of how many lights a room has? Can a guest control their lighting without any prior explanation? And does the room stay fully functional when there is no network connection? These are the risks that could invalidate the product entirely. They need to be settled first, before anything else is designed or built.

---

## Requirements Validated by the Prototype
REQ-ID | Requirement | What the prototype proves

- F-REQ-01 | **Lighting Zone Tagging** | That categorizing lights by function rather than by identity actually solves the variable-room problem, the same theme produces the right atmosphere whether the room has 4 lights or 12.
- F-REQ-02 | **Brand Theme Creation and Global Sync** | That a hotel can define its brand atmosphere once and push it to a room from a central place, the global rollout is assumed to work the same way, just at scale.
- F-REQ-03 | **Guest Flex Mode** | That guests can personalize their environment and return to the hotel preset without confusion or frustration.
- F-REQ-04 | **Bedside Master Off Control** | That the single most common guest complaint, having to get out of bed to turn off the lights, is now fully resolved.

- NF-REQ-01 | **Offline Local Operation** | That a network or cloud outage does not affect the guest experience in any way.
- NF-REQ-04 | **Minimal Guest Interface** | That a guest can use the controls correctly without instructions beyond a single sentence.
